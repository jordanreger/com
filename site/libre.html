libre
/'li:bɹə/

borrowed from french/spanish (both derived from the latin root "liber"), libre means (to be) free, unrestricted. [source]


unrestricted?
the sources of all my code is under the MIT license and the content is under the CC BY-NC 4.0 license (unless otherwise stated). all of my content is free to access, copy, redistribute, remix, transform, build upon, share, call your own, whatever you'd like - unless you're doing it commercially. you may say, "isn't this restricting?", and... well, yes, it is. however, this is purely in an attempt to keep a free thing free. if you'd like to discuss licensing a portion of my work, email me!

despite the commerical restriction, everything i create is available for reuse. if you happen to like my site, use it for yourself! here's the source.


free
everything i do in my personal life is free. all of my work, art and information is available for free use and enjoy. by keeping everything free, it allows anyone to access it. as of now, my contributions are exclusively online - but not forever.
however, if you'd like to support me for any reason, you can donate via liberapay.


open access
the open access of information is extremely important to me. as a believer in science, i know that the truth is always changing because our understanding of reality is always changing. this is because our observations (through technology, like JWST, or through knowing where to look) or our methods are getting better all the time. however, by spreading misinformation, denying truthful information (and science by extension), by closing access to information or actively redacting it, open access to truthful information becomes limited and as a whole our society gets worse. i feel like a lot of problems and misunderstandings we have as a society are due to misinformation or a lack of true information. to combat this, we should explore and create in libre foundations in mind. here's to exploring, documenting and creating for everyone.
